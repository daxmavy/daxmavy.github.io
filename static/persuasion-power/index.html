Power and cost: three-arm persuasion experiment¶

Statistical model, design assumptions, and power/cost results for an experiment with three summary arms — against / neutral / for. The model and variance components are taken from Röttger et al.'s ai-distortion persuasion follow-up (Study 5), refit here to validate consistency.

1. Model and assumptions¶

Statistical model (identical to Paul's persuasion_regressions_full_analyses.R):

$$\text{stance\_shift} \;\sim\; \text{arm} \;+\; (1\mid\text{reader}) \;+\; (1\mid\text{proposition})$$

a Gaussian linear mixed model (glmmTMB), crossed random intercepts for reader and proposition. The outcome is the oriented stance shift $\text{direction}\times(\text{attitude}_{\text{post}}-\text{attitude}_{\text{pre}})$ on the 0–100 policy-attitude scale, where direction orients towards the arm's advocated position. arm has levels {neutral (reference), for, against}.

Estimand. The for-vs-neutral fixed-effect coefficient $\Delta$ (biased-vs-neutral mean shift). Results are reported across assumed $\Delta \in \{1, 2, 5, 10\}$ points.

Design. Within-participant; each participant reads 6 paragraphs — 2 per arm.

Proposition pool. 12 propositions (selection rule in §4).

Power. 90% power, two-sided $\alpha = 0.05$. The SE of the for-vs-neutral coefficient — which is identical to its marginaleffects AME standard error, i.e. computed the same way as Paul's effects (§2, §3) — is obtained by simulating the model above with the fitted variance components over the 12-proposition pool; it scales as $SE(N) = SE_{\text{unit}}/\sqrt{N_{\text{readers}}}$, giving $n = \big((z_{1-\alpha/2}+z_{\text{power}})\,SE_{\text{unit}}/\Delta\big)^2$.

Cost model (Paul's): £12 per rater-hour (£9 + 33% Prolific fee); 1.5 min per paragraph + 2.5 min fixed overhead per rater.

2. Validation against Paul's data¶

The model is refit on data/followup_persuasion with glmmTMB. Point estimates, standard errors, and 95% confidence intervals for the average marginal effects and condition means are computed via marginaleffects (delta method, type = "response", re.form = NA) — Paul's exact procedure — and compared with the values committed in results/followup_persuasion. (Paul reports CIs and z-statistics; his SE is recovered as estimate/statistic for the AMEs and as CI-width/$2z_{.975}$ for the condition means.) Requires R + glmmTMB + marginaleffects; script: replicate_paul.R.

        'AME: AI vs human': 'paragraph_type_ai_vs_human'}
cmap = {f'mean shift: {c}': c for c in ['control','human-low','human-high','ai-low','ai-high']}
def paul_row(q):
    if q in amap:
        r = ame.loc[amap[q]]
        return pd.Series({'paul_est': r['ame'], 'paul_se': r['ame']/r['statistic'],
                          'paul_ci_low': r['ame_low'], 'paul_ci_high': r['ame_high']})
    if q in cmap:
        r = cm.loc[cmap[q]]
        return pd.Series({'paul_est': r['mean_stance_shift'],
                          'paul_se': (r['conf.high']-r['conf.low'])/(2*Z),
                          'paul_ci_low': r['conf.low'], 'paul_ci_high': r['conf.high']})
    return pd.Series({'paul_est': np.nan, 'paul_se': np.nan, 'paul_ci_low': np.nan, 'paul_ci_high': np.nan})
out = pd.concat([mine, mine.quantity.apply(paul_row)], axis=1)
out['|Δ est|'] = (out.computed - out.paul_est).abs()
out['|Δ SE|'] = (out.se - out.paul_se).abs()
out['|Δ CI|'] = np.maximum((out.ci_low - out.paul_ci_low).abs(), (out.ci_high - out.paul_ci_high).abs())

3. Variance components (power inputs)¶

From the refit RQ2 model (computed in §2):

component SD (points)
reader 2.94
proposition 1.92
residual 15.87
marginal 16.25

Simulating the three-arm design under the model with these components over the 12-proposition pool gives $SE_{\text{unit}} = 15.85$ for the for-vs-neutral coefficient (sim_power.R). That coefficient's SE equals its marginaleffects AME standard error (verified identical to $10^{-6}$), so the uncertainty driving the power calculation is computed exactly as in §2. At the N predicted for 90% power at $\Delta=3$ (N = 293), 200 simulated experiments reject the null in 92% of cases (validate_power.R).
